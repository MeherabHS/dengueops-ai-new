# DengueOps AI — Demo Video Script

**Target duration:** 3–5 minutes  
**Audience:** IEEE ICADHI judges, MSc scholarship reviewers  
**Tone:** Professional, clear, methodically confident

---

## Scene 1: Title Card (0:00–0:15)

*[Show: Project title card against navy background]*

**Narration:**
"DengueOps AI — Simulation-Based Dengue Surge Preparedness Decision Support for Dhaka South.
A submission to IEEE ICADHI 2026, Track 06: Health Data Analytics and Predictive Systems."

---

## Scene 2: Problem (0:15–0:45)

*[Show: Home page — Problem section]*

**Narration:**
"Dengue surges in Dhaka South are met reactively. Facilities run out of NS1 test kits.
Beds fill up before procurement is triggered. The gap isn't the absence of forecasts —
it's the absence of a framework to convert those forecasts into preparedness intelligence.

DengueOps AI addresses that gap."

---

## Scene 3: Innovation Framing (0:45–1:10)

*[Show: Home page — Innovation section, quoting the core positioning statement]*

**Narration:**
"DengueOps AI does not claim a novel forecasting algorithm. Its contribution is the
operational decision-support layer — a simulation-based preparedness engine that converts
lag-aware dengue forecasts into supply depletion timelines, bed pressure estimates,
zone priority scores, and actionable directives."

---

## Scene 4: Dashboard Overview (1:10–2:00)

*[Show: Dashboard page — top metric cards and scenario selector]*

**Narration:**
"The dashboard shows the current forecast: 1,200 cases forecasted for Epi Week 24,
a 2.1× growth factor versus baseline, a Very high forecast growth category, and an Experimental Growth Score of 88 out of 100. The score is provisional, not a probability or validated risk score.

The scenario selector lets planners toggle between best-case, expected, and worst-case
projections — giving a range of preparedness requirements rather than a single point estimate."

---

## Scene 5: Supply & Bed Metrics (2:00–2:45)

*[Show: Supply Depletion chart, Bed Gap chart, Facility Readiness table]*

**Narration:**
"For each facility, the system computes a Supply Depletion Horizon — the number of days
until NS1 kits and IV fluids run out under surge demand. Kamrangirchar is at 5 days —
a critical threshold. The bed gap chart shows which zones will face a bed deficit.

The facility readiness table gives a single-screen view of all critical preparedness
gaps across five operational zones."

---

## Scene 6: Zone Priority & Directives (2:45–3:30)

*[Show: Zone Risk table, Directive cards]*

**Narration:**
"Zones are ranked by priority score — a composite of exposure index and vulnerability weight.
Kamrangirchar ranks first with a score of 1.16.

For each zone, the system generates structured directives — tiered by severity.
Kamrangirchar's directives include: emergency bed protocol activation, NS1 kit resupply
within 48 hours, and patient overflow coordination. All directives are advisory only —
they require qualified public health professional review."

---

## Scene 7: Methodology & Validation (3:30–4:00)

*[Show: Methodology page — formula cards; Validation page — metric cards]*

**Narration:**
"The methodology page documents the four core formulas — Supply Depletion Horizon,
Dynamic Daily Demand, Projected Bed Load, and Priority Score — along with the
14- and 28-day climate lag rationale and walk-forward backtest design.

The validation page shows MAE of 42.3, RMSE of 61.8, and MAPE of 14.7%,
outperforming three baselines on the held-out test set."

---

## Scene 8: Ethics & Transparency (4:00–4:20)

*[Show: Ethics page and Assumptions page]*

**Narration:**
"DengueOps AI is built with explicit ethical safeguards: aggregated and synthetic data only,
no patient-level processing, human-in-the-loop decision-making by design, and full assumption
disclosure. The assumptions page documents every known limitation."

---

## Scene 9: Closing (4:20–4:45)

*[Show: About page, then home page CTA]*

**Narration:**
"DengueOps AI is Phase 0 of a scalable public health decision-support architecture.
Phase 1 will integrate validated DGHS surveillance feeds, real facility data,
and a production analytics pipeline.

The contribution is clear: a transparent, methodically sound framework for converting
outbreak forecasts into operational preparedness intelligence — designed for
data-scarce urban health settings where these tools are needed most."

---

*[End card: Project title, IEEE ICADHI 2026, Track 06]*
