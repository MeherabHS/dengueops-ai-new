# DengueOps AI — Ethics Statement

## Summary

DengueOps AI is a public health planning aid, not an autonomous decision-making system.
It is designed to support — not replace — professional judgement of public health administrators,
epidemiologists, and facility managers.

---

## 1. No Patient-Level Data

DengueOps AI does not collect, process, store, or transmit any patient-level, individual-level,
or de-identified personal health data at any stage.

All inputs to the system are:
- Aggregated epidemiological surveillance data (weekly case counts at city level)
- Aggregated climate data (rainfall, humidity, temperature)
- Synthetic data generated for demonstration purposes

**Phase 0 uses synthetic/placeholder data only.**

---

## 2. Aggregated & Synthetic Data Only

The Phase 0 prototype operates exclusively on:
- Manually compiled aggregate public health data
- Computationally generated synthetic data

No electronic health records, hospital management system data, personal identifiers, or
any form of individual-level data is accessed, processed, or stored.

---

## 3. No Clinical Diagnosis or Triage

DengueOps AI is explicitly **not** a clinical decision-support tool.

It does **not**:
- Diagnose dengue fever in individual patients
- Triage patients for treatment
- Make recommendations about individual patient care
- Replace any clinical assessment or medical examination

All outputs relate to **population-level preparedness planning** for public health administrators.

---

## 4. Human-in-the-Loop Decision-Making

All operational directives generated by DengueOps AI are **advisory only**.

The system explicitly requires:
- Qualified public health professionals to review all outputs
- Facility administrators to validate capacity assessments
- Health authority approval before any preparedness actions

**No automated actions are initiated or recommended.**

---

## 5. Public Health Planning Focus

DengueOps AI is designed for aggregate public health planning:
- Supply procurement decision support
- Bed capacity anticipation
- Zone-level resource prioritisation
- Vector control deployment coordination

It is **not** designed for:
- Clinical management
- Patient tracking
- Healthcare billing
- Individual-level health monitoring

---

## 6. Data Privacy Safeguards

- No data infrastructure collects or persists user inputs in Phase 0
- The prototype is a static, read-only showcase application
- Phase 1+ data integration will require formal data governance agreements
- Any real-world deployment requires data sharing protocols with DGHS/IEDCR or DSCC

---

## 7. Bias & Vulnerability Safeguards

The spatial exposure heuristic explicitly incorporates a **vulnerability weight** to:
- Prevent systematic underallocation to high-density informal settlement zones
- Account for differential healthcare access across zones

**Current limitation:** The vulnerability weight is an approximation.
Systematic validation against ground truth data is required before operational use.

---

## 8. Safety Limitations

DengueOps AI produces approximate projections based on simplified assumptions.

Overconfidence in outputs without validation could lead to:
- Misallocation of limited health resources
- Inadequate preparation in underweighted zones
- False reassurance if scenarios underestimate true surge magnitude

The system is **advisory only**. All outputs must be interpreted by qualified professionals
with knowledge of the local health context.

---

## Responsible Deployment Requirements

Before any operational deployment, the following are required:
1. Formal data governance agreement with relevant health authorities
2. Validation of all assumptions against local ground truth data
3. Independent evaluation of model performance on local historical data
4. Training of users on system limitations and advisory nature
5. Institutional oversight and review process
